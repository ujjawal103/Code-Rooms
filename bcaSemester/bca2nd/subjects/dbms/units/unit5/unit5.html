Concurrency Control Overview

Concurrency control ensures that database transactions are executed in a safe and consistent manner in a multi-user environment. This technique prevents conflicts, anomalies, and data inconsistency that may arise when multiple transactions are performed simultaneously.

The main goal of concurrency control is to allow transactions to be executed concurrently without affecting the correctness of the database. Different protocols are used to achieve this goal, such as lock-based protocols, timestamp-based protocols, and validation-based protocols.
